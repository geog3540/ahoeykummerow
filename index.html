Households with No People Under 18 in U.S. States

This graduated symbol map shows the number of households without children under 18 across U.S. states using scaled circles. Larger and darker symbols represent states with more child-free households, with the largest concentrations in populous states like California, Texas, Florida, and New York.

Percent of People Over 25 with Bachelor's Degrees by Sex in Kansas Counties

This bivariate choropleth map visualizes the educational attainment of people over 25 across Kansas counties. Purple tones indicate counties where women have higher degree attainment, while teal tones represent higher rates among men. Blue tones show where both sexes have higher attainment levels.

Yearly Total Precipitation in Iowa Counties

This isarithmic map visualizes yearly total precipitation across Iowa using contour lines and color gradients. Northern and northeastern counties receive the highest rainfall, as shown by the darker blue tones, while southern and western areas tend to be drier.

Family Migration in U.S. States from 1887 to 1924

This historical flow map displays net family migration across the United States between 1887 and 1924, with arrows showing migration paths and circle sizes representing total migration activity. The choropleth layer highlights net migration, with darker greens indicating higher net in-migration and deeper pinks showing net out-migration. Oklahoma emerges as a major migration destination, reflecting settlement incentives during this period.

Final Project: Heat Vulnerability Index Distribution in Portland, Oregon

For this project, we created a Heat Vulnerability Index (HVI) using six variables: impervious surface cover, canopy density, elevation, population density, poverty rates, and percent population of vulnerable age. This environmental and social data helps pinpoint neighborhoods most at risk. We then analyzed access to cooling resources and found that over half of these high-risk areas lack nearby parks. The final visualizations are presented in an interactive ArcGIS StoryMap designed to educate users and promote climate resilience planning.
